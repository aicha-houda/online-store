Discover how to make the most of your Noulika perfumes with proper application, storage, and longevity tips.

Common Perfume Questions

How should I apply my perfume?

Spray perfume on pulse points (wrists, neck, behind ears) from about 6 inches away. Avoid rubbing as this can break down fragrance molecules. For best results, apply to moisturized skin.

Why doesn't my perfume last all day?

Longevity depends on concentration (EDT, EDP, etc.), skin type, and environment. Oily skin holds fragrance longer. Try applying to clothing (test first) or carrying a travel spray for reapplication.

Can I return a perfume if I don't like the scent?

Due to hygiene reasons, we can only accept returns of unopened, sealed perfumes with original packaging within 30 days. We recommend trying samples first.

How should I store my perfume?

Keep in original box away from light and heat. Avoid bathroom storage due to humidity fluctuations. Ideal temperature is 15-25°C (59-77°F).

Does perfume expire?

Properly stored perfumes can last 3-5 years. Signs of expiration include color change, strange odor, or separation. Citrus notes degrade fastest.

Perfume Application Guide

Prep Your Skin

Apply to clean, moisturized skin. Unscented lotion creates an ideal base. Avoid areas with jewelry that may tarnish.

Spray Technique

Hold bottle 6 inches from skin. Spray in a sweeping motion across pulse points. 1-2 sprays per area is sufficient.

Layering

Use matching shower gel and lotion first. Apply perfume last. Reapply lotion during the day to refresh scent.

Eau de Toilette

3-5 hours

Eau de Parfum

5-8 hours
(15-20% perfume oil)

Parfum

8+ hours
(20-30% perfume oil)

Proper Perfume Storage

Avoid Light

Store in original box or dark cabinet to prevent UV degradation

Control Temperature

Keep in cool, stable environment (not bathroom or car)

Seal Tightly

Always recap bottles to prevent evaporation and oxidation

Original Packaging

Boxes protect from light and temperature fluctuations
